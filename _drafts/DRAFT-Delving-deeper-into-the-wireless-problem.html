With some help from the nice folk at the DD-WRT forums, I did some further troubleshooting.  Turns out the ping spikes do not happen over a wired connection, and hey happen - here's the big part - with any wifi router I try.

This meant signal interference.  Something, somewhere was causing a burst of interference every 90 seconds. I gave up to cry under my bed.  Signal interference is a bitch to pin down, under the best of circumstances!

Then I moved apartments. I now live in a farm house, literally in the middle of nowhere.  I am the only wireless provider in range - indeed, the nearest village is more than 5km away.  Sweet, sweet wireless silence.  So I ran my ping test again... and I still get dropouts every 90 seconds.

This means that *I* am the cause of the interference.  Something I own bursts wifi interference every 90 seconds.  I'm now running around my house, frantically shutting down everything I can to figure out what is causing the problem.

With all my electronics turned off, even my iphone... I still get the ping drops.
